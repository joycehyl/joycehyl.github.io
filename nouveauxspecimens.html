Extinction is the termination of a species when its last individual dies out. More than 99% of all species that ever lived on Earth, amounting to over five billion species, are estimated to have died out. You may know the term from extinctions of the DoDo Bird or the mammoth, yet report finds that around 1,000,000,000 animal and plant species are now threatened with extinction, many within decades, more than ever before in human history. Sadly, there is no going back once they are gone. Even with statistics saying so, our range of knowledge is still limited to the discovered species, ignorant of the undiscovered that may also be dying out.

In fear that much of the species may not live up to our future generations,
nouveaux specimens aims to preserve a digital archive for obscured species. Here you may find some of our proudest specimens, including actual images and footages caught from these rarely seen species. With nouveaux specimens, there is no longer the need to worry the extinction of species for our lab holds an entire database to document their existence.
